## CATEGORY 1: OPENING/RAPPORT (Questions 1-20)

### Q1-5: Icebreakers (Friendly Start)

**Q1: "How did you first discover [Obsidian/PKM/note-taking]?"**
- Listen for: Organic discovery vs intentional search
- Probe: What problem were you trying to solve?
- Reveals: Motivation level, problem awareness

**Q2: "What's your favorite thing about your current note-taking setup?"**
- Listen for: What they value most
- Probe: Why is that important to you?
- Reveals: Core needs, non-negotiables

**Q3: "If you could show off one thing in your vault to a friend, what would it be?"**
- Listen for: Pride points, what they consider success
- Probe: What makes it special to you?
- Reveals: Values, aesthetic vs functional preferences

**Q4: "What's the weirdest note you have in your vault?"**
- Listen for: Personality, use case diversity
- Probe: Do you have lots of notes like that?
- Reveals: Comfort level, vault breadth

**Q5: "When did you realize you needed a 'system' for your notes vs just taking notes?"**
- Listen for: Tipping point moment
- Probe: What happened that made you realize this?
- Reveals: Problem severity, catalyst event

### Q6-10: Background Discovery (Understanding Context)

**Q6: "Walk me through a typical day of note-taking for you."**
- Listen for: Frequency, contexts, variety
- Probe: What triggers you to take a note?
- Reveals: Use patterns, capture habits

**Q7: "What percentage of your notes are for work vs personal use?"**
- Listen for: Primary use case
- Probe: Do you mix them or separate?
- Reveals: Privacy needs, organizational complexity

**Q8: "How many different types of notes do you take? (meetings, ideas, research, etc.)"**
- Listen for: Complexity of needs
- Probe: Which type is hardest to manage?
- Reveals: Specific pain points by category

**Q9: "Do you ever go back and read your old notes?"**
- Listen for: Retrieval patterns vs write-only behavior
- Probe: How do you find notes when you need them?
- Reveals: Search vs browse, organization importance

**Q10: "What's the oldest note in your vault that you still reference?"**
- Listen for: Long-term value, archival needs
- Probe: What makes it still relevant?
- Reveals: Evergreen content, organizational longevity
