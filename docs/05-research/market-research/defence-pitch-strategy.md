## V. Risk Mitigation & Contingency Plans

### A. Common Pitch Failure Modes

#### **Failure Mode 1: "We'll think about it" (No follow-up)**
**Prevention:**
- Always end meetings with specific next action and deadline
- Get calendar invites scheduled before leaving the room
- Provide lightweight next step (15-min call, not 2-hour workshop)

**Recovery:**
- Send personalized follow-up within 24 hours
- Include specific value based on their stated pain points
- Offer to connect them with reference customer (if available)

---

#### **Failure Mode 2: "Too technical, we don't understand"**
**Prevention:**
- Tailor demo complexity to audience (executive = simple, analyst = detailed)
- Use analogies: "Like Google Maps for cyber threats"
- Provide visual aids: diagrams, flowcharts, not code

**Recovery:**
- Offer to re-present with technical staff present
- Send simplified explainer video
- Schedule office hours for Q&A

---

#### **Failure Mode 3: "Your competitor just pitched us something similar"**
**Prevention:**
- Proactively differentiate early in pitch
- Name competitors explicitly: "Unlike Palantir which..."
- Focus on unique capabilities (graph attribution, ITIL native)

**Recovery:**
- Send competitive comparison matrix
- Offer side-by-side proof-of-concept
- Emphasize Canadian ownership and data sovereignty

---

#### **Failure Mode 4: "We need approval from [higher authority]"**
**Prevention:**
- Identify true decision-maker before formal pitch
- Build coalition of champions (analyst + procurement + executive)
- Provide leave-behind materials designed for upward briefing

**Recovery:**
- Offer to brief decision-maker directly
- Provide executive summary (1-pager) champion can forward
- Leverage prime partner to make introduction at exec level
